AAPI History Hub — Partnership Workflow Playbook

Step-by-step outreach and engagement workflows by partner type

Who this is for

Museums, cultural institutions, curriculum creators, and organizations that produce AAPI educational content — e.g., Wing Luke Museum, SAADA, OCA National, Advancing Justice, Yuri Project, Facing History, Gilder Lehrman.

1
Initial Outreach
Email or LinkedIn contact to introduce the Hub
›
  • Research their existing content catalog and any AAPI focus areas before reaching out
  • Identify the right contact — programs director, education lead, or partnerships team
  • Keep the initial message brief: introduce TAAF and the Hub, note a specific piece of their content that fits well
Sample message

"Hi [Name], I lead partnerships for the AAPI History Hub, a free educator resource from the Tipping Point Community. We've been looking at [Org]'s work on [specific topic] — it aligns closely with what teachers are searching for on our platform. I'd love to share more and explore whether featuring your content could expand your reach into classrooms nationwide. Would you have 30 minutes in the next few weeks?"

2
Discovery Meeting
Learn about their content, goals, and capacity
›
  • Ask about their content priorities and what topics they most want educators to engage with
  • Understand their update cadence — how frequently do they release new material?
  • Learn about any existing partnerships with schools or districts
  • Gauge their capacity to share updates with the Hub team as content changes
  • Ask: Do they have distribution channels (newsletters, socials) that could amplify a Hub announcement?
Key question: "What would make this feel like a genuine partnership rather than just a listing?"
3
Share the Hub
Walk them through the platform and show their content in context
›
● Universal first step for all partner types
  • Give a live demo — show how teachers discover and filter content
  • Show them how their content would appear with tags, grade levels, and subject filters
  • Highlight the credibility and quality signal their inclusion provides to educators
  • Walk through how content is featured in Hub newsletters and marketing
4
Propose Next Steps
Tailor the partnership based on what you learned
›
Feature their content
Host or link their resources on the Hub with full attribution and tags
Highlight in marketing
Feature their organization in Hub newsletters, social posts, or TAAF Summit
State toolkit collaboration
If regional: co-curate a state-specific toolkit or learning guide
Deeper exploration
Co-create a unit, virtual field trip companion, or educator discount access
If they have a local educator network or district connections, ask about warm intros to amplify reach
Who this is for

Teacher networks, unions, affinity groups, and professional communities — e.g., NEA, AFT, ACTFL, Networks of Independent Schools, pre-service credentialing programs, and local educator affinity groups.

1
Initial Outreach
Reach through membership or programs leadership
›
  • Target the professional development, curriculum, or diversity/equity lead within the network
  • If there is an AAPI affinity group or caucus, reach out to them directly as a warm entry point
  • Reference the network's mission and how the Hub can serve their teacher members
  • Offer to present at an upcoming meeting or share a resource spotlight in their newsletter
Sample message

"Hi [Name], I'm reaching out from the AAPI History Hub, a free classroom resource platform from TAAF. Given [Network]'s commitment to culturally relevant teaching, I think the Hub could be a valuable resource for your members — and we'd love to explore how to share it with your community. Could we connect for a quick call?"

2
Discovery Meeting
Understand how they support members and share resources
›
  • Ask how teachers in the network currently find and share classroom resources
  • Learn about their PD calendar — are there regular convenings where the Hub could be introduced?
  • Inquire about any AAPI caucus, affinity group, or cultural heritage programming
  • Understand their advocacy channels — newsletter, social media, regional chapters
  • Ask if any members have used the Hub and could become Hub Heroes (champions)
Key question: "How do you currently surface new teaching resources to members — and what makes one stick?"
3
Share the Hub
Demo the platform with a teacher's eye
›
● Universal first step for all partner types
  • Frame the demo from a classroom teacher's perspective — discovery, filtering, lesson integration
  • Show grade-level and subject filtering to reflect their membership's needs
  • Highlight any content aligned to standards they care about (state standards, universal competencies)
  • Share examples of teachers already using the Hub in their classrooms
4
Propose Next Steps
Build toward endorsement and network-wide sharing
›
Share with members
Newsletter feature, email blast, or resource spotlight to their educator community
AAPI affinity group connection
Connect directly with AAPI caucus or affinity group for deeper engagement and advocacy
Identify Hub Heroes
Recruit passionate member teachers to champion the Hub in their school communities
PD integration
Explore embedding the Hub into existing PD sessions or member convenings
National networks: prioritize endorsement and advocacy. Regional/local: prioritize direct member sharing and Hub Hero identification
Who this is for

School districts, county offices of education, and state education agencies in focus regions — e.g., SFUSD, OCDE, Alliance CRPS (CA), AAPI NJ, Wing Luke connections (WA). Focus regions 2026: CA, NJ, WA.

1
Initial Outreach
Reach the curriculum, DEI, or social studies lead
›
  • Identify the right door: curriculum director, social studies coordinator, DEI/equity lead, or superintendent's office
  • Reference any existing state laws or mandates around AAPI history instruction as a hook
  • Leverage warm introductions from content partners, community orgs, or Hub Heroes in the region when available
  • Lead with the free, classroom-ready value — no budget required to use the Hub
Sample message

"Hi [Name], I'm reaching out from the AAPI History Hub, a free educator platform from TAAF. With [State]'s growing focus on inclusive history instruction, we're connecting with districts to share the Hub and explore how we can support your teachers. Would you be open to a 30-minute call to learn more and give us feedback?"

2
Discovery Meeting
Understand their curriculum landscape and adoption pathways
›
  • Ask about current approaches to AAPI history and ethnic studies instruction
  • Understand how new resources get vetted and shared with teachers — district resource lists, instructional coaches, etc.
  • Learn about any AAPI staff affinity groups or community advisory boards
  • Ask about PD calendar and whether the Hub could be introduced at a staff training day
  • Identify whether they have a relationship with state standards bodies or curriculum adoption cycles
Key question: "If a teacher wanted to use a new resource from the Hub next semester, what would need to happen for that to be supported by the district?"
3
Share the Hub
Demo with a district administrator's lens
›
● Universal first step for all partner types
  • Emphasize standards alignment and the vetting/quality assurance process behind Hub content
  • Show state-specific filtering and any existing content relevant to their region
  • Highlight the "Start Here" guides and parent-facing resources for broader community engagement
  • Mention how other districts are using the Hub (case studies, Hub Heroes)
4
Propose Next Steps
Design a path toward system-wide adoption
›
Add to district resource list
Get the Hub onto approved teacher resource or curriculum supplement lists
AAPI affinity group connection
If they have AAPI staff or community groups, connect directly for deeper buy-in
Staff PD presentation
Offer a Hub overview session at a professional development day or all-staff meeting
State learning guide collaboration
Co-create a state-specific curriculum guide or toolkit aligned to their standards
Pilot or case study: invite one school or grade-level team to formally pilot the Hub and document classroom impact — this builds the evidence base for district-wide expansion
Who this is for

Professional development organizations, teacher training providers, and school support organizations — e.g., New Teacher Center, Leading Educators, TNTP, National Equity Project, Teaching for Justice, teacher colleges and credentialing programs.

1
Initial Outreach
Connect through program design or curriculum leadership
›
  • Target the curriculum design lead, program director, or content team — those who shape what goes into PD sessions
  • Reference how the Hub complements their existing instructional frameworks (culturally responsive teaching, equity, social studies)
  • Position the Hub as a turnkey content layer they can build PD around — not a competitor
  • Mention TAAF's credibility and any shared organizational partners
Sample message

"Hi [Name], I work on partnerships for the AAPI History Hub — a vetted, free resource platform for educators from TAAF. We're exploring whether the Hub could be a useful complement to [Org]'s PD work. A number of your partner districts are already exploring it, and we think there's a real opportunity to co-design something that embeds the Hub into teacher training. Could we find 30 minutes to explore what that might look like?"

2
Discovery Meeting
Understand their PD model and teacher audience
›
  • Learn about their core PD offerings — live sessions, async modules, coaching cycles
  • Ask which content areas (social studies, ethnic studies, ELA) and grade bands are their primary focus
  • Understand their current approach to culturally responsive content — any gaps in AAPI materials?
  • Ask about their teacher network scale — how many educators do they reach annually?
  • Explore what a co-branded or co-designed offering might look like for them
Key question: "If you were designing a PD session tomorrow that featured the Hub, what would you want it to accomplish for teachers?"
3
Share the Hub
Demo through the lens of a PD designer
›
● Universal first step for all partner types
  • Show how the Hub can anchor a PD session — e.g., explore 3 resources, discuss classroom application
  • Highlight the professional learning (PL) page and how partner PD offerings are featured there
  • Walk through content aligned to universal teacher competencies they may already use
  • Show the standards-alignment and filtering tools that make session planning easier
4
Propose Next Steps
Co-design a PD offering that features the Hub
›
Feature on the PL page
List their PD offerings on the Hub's professional learning page with referral links
Co-design a PD session
Collaborate on a live or async session that uses Hub content as the primary resource
Professional learning module
Co-create a standalone Hub-focused PD module they can embed in coaching cycles or courses
Credentialing integration
For teacher colleges: explore embedding Hub resources into pre-service coursework or student teacher placements
Pilot goal: a co-designed PD session, piloted with one cohort, with a case study capturing teacher feedback and resource adoption rates — this becomes a replicable model for other PD partners
Who this is for

Conferences, associations, and events organizations that reach educators or AAPI communities at scale — e.g., NCSS, National Equity Project, Teaching for Justice, CAAPLE, CA Social Science Conference, SXSW EDU, state and regional educator conferences.

1
Initial Outreach
Connect with programming or partnerships lead
›
  • Reach out to the conference programming director or sponsorship/partnerships contact
  • Review their past program for sessions on AAPI content, ethnic studies, or culturally relevant teaching
  • Propose specific value exchanges upfront: TAAF speaker, session content, or co-sponsorship interest
  • For large national conferences (NCSS, SXSW EDU), approach with a full proposal; for regional events, a lighter-touch email works
Sample message

"Hi [Name], I lead partnerships for TAAF's AAPI History Hub — a free educator resource platform reaching teachers nationwide. We're excited about [Conference] and would love to explore how we might contribute: whether that's a session featuring Hub resources, a TAAF-sponsored speaker, or another form of partnership. Would you have time to connect?"

2
Discovery Meeting
Learn about their audience, format, and partnership needs
›
  • Understand their attendee profile — grade levels, subject areas, geographic reach
  • Learn about session proposal timelines and what makes a compelling pitch for their audience
  • Ask about existing sponsorship tiers and what visibility options exist for TAAF
  • Explore whether they distribute pre- or post-event resources to attendees — a natural channel for the Hub
  • Ask about AAPI Heritage Month programming or any cultural/ethnic studies focus areas
Key question: "What's the most effective way a partner like TAAF has contributed to your conference in the past — and what would be most useful to you this year?"
3
Share the Hub
Frame it as a resource their attendees will love
›
● Universal first step for all partner types
  • Emphasize the Hub as a free, high-quality resource their educator attendees can start using immediately
  • Show how it could be introduced in a session, featured in a conference app, or included in attendee email follow-ups
  • Mention the TAAF Summit as a potential cross-promotion opportunity
  • Offer to provide Hub content or TAAF speakers to enrich their programming
4
Propose Next Steps
Secure a presence at the event and build toward ongoing visibility
›
Session proposal / speaking slot
Submit a session featuring Hub content and a TAAF speaker or content creator
Attendee communication
Pre- or post-event email to attendees featuring the Hub, sent through the organizer
Sponsorship / exhibit presence
Explore existing sponsorship tiers for Hub visibility at the event
Co-create new opportunity
For high-priority events: design a new sponsorship type or co-branded session series
Cross-promote with the TAAF Summit: invite conference speakers or organizers to present, and use the Hub as a shared resource bridge between communities
